# Building Your Dream: The Rea Co Homes Custom Building Process in Central Oregon

Central Oregon is a region defined by breathtaking landscapes, vibrant communities, and unparalleled luxury living. Whether you envision a modern mountain retreat in **Tetherow**, a sprawling estate overlooking the high desert at **Brasada Ranch**, or a sophisticated home near the amenities of **Broken Top** in Bend, the journey to building a custom home is an exciting, yet complex, endeavor.

At Rea Co Homes, we understand that complexity. For over 45 years, our founder, Kevin Rea, has been perfecting the art and science of custom home construction. Our process is designed not just to build a structure, but to create a seamless, transparent, and enjoyable experience that culminates in a home perfectly tailored to your vision. Building a luxury home requires precision, deep local knowledge, and unwavering dedication—qualities that define the Rea Co Homes standard.

Here is a detailed look at the five essential phases of the Rea Co Homes custom building process.

## Phase 1: Vision and Pre-Construction Planning

This initial phase is arguably the most crucial. It’s where your dreams take concrete form and where the foundation for a successful project budget and timeline is laid.

### A. Discovery and Site Evaluation

We begin with a deep dive into your lifestyle, aesthetic preferences, and functional needs. Simultaneously, we conduct a thorough evaluation of your chosen homesite. Building in areas like **Pronghorn** or **Sunriver** often involves unique environmental considerations, HOA requirements, and specific design guidelines. Kevin Rea’s decades of experience ensure that we anticipate potential challenges—from geological surveys to utility access—long before ground is broken.

### B. Design Team Assembly and Collaboration

We collaborate closely with top architects and designers who specialize in Central Oregon’s distinct architectural styles. This collaborative approach ensures that the design is not only beautiful but also practical and buildable within your financial parameters. We focus on value engineering early on, optimizing materials and methods to achieve high quality without unnecessary cost escalation.

### C. Budgeting and Contract Finalization

Transparency is paramount. We develop a detailed, line-item budget based on finalized plans and specifications. Our open-book approach ensures you understand exactly where every dollar is allocated. Once the budget and construction schedule are approved, we finalize the contract, providing a clear roadmap for the entire project.

## Phase 2: Permitting and Site Preparation

Before construction can begin, meticulous administrative and physical preparation is required.

### A. Permitting and Approvals

Navigating the permitting process in Central Oregon—especially within specific communities like **Tetherow** or **Brasada Ranch** with strict architectural review boards—can be time-consuming. Rea Co Homes manages all required municipal, county, and HOA approvals, leveraging our established relationships to streamline this critical step.

### B. Site Logistics and Preparation

Site preparation involves clearing, grading, and establishing essential infrastructure. This includes setting up temporary utilities, ensuring proper erosion control (vital for protecting the sensitive Central Oregon environment), and establishing a secure, organized construction site. A well-prepared site is the first sign of a professionally managed project.

## Phase 3: Foundation and Framing (The Structural Core)

This phase transforms the prepared site into the tangible outline of your future home.

### A. Foundation and Utilities

Pouring the foundation is a moment of truth. Given Central Oregon’s variable climate and soil conditions, precision is non-negotiable. We ensure the foundation is robust, properly insulated, and seamlessly integrated with initial utility rough-ins (plumbing, electrical, and HVAC).

### B. Framing and Roofing

Framing defines the volume, flow, and character of the home. Our skilled framing crews adhere to the highest structural standards. Once the structure is complete, the roof is installed, achieving 'dry-in.' This critical milestone protects the structure from the elements, allowing interior work to commence regardless of the weather in **Bend** or **Sunriver**.

## Phase 4: Mechanical, Electrical, and Interior Rough-Ins

With the structure enclosed, the focus shifts to the complex systems that make the home functional, comfortable, and smart.

### A. Systems Installation

This is where the 'guts' of the home are installed: HVAC ducts, electrical wiring, plumbing pipes, and low-voltage systems (security, audio/visual). Modern luxury homes often feature sophisticated systems, and Rea Co Homes ensures these are installed meticulously, meeting or exceeding code requirements.

### B. Inspections and Insulation

Multiple inspections occur during this phase to verify that all systems meet safety and quality standards before they are concealed by drywall. Following inspections, high-performance insulation is installed. Given Central Oregon’s temperature swings, superior insulation is key to energy efficiency and comfort.

### C. Interior Finishes Selection

While rough-ins are underway, homeowners work with our team and designers to finalize all interior finishes—cabinetry, flooring, tile, paint colors, lighting fixtures, and custom millwork. Clear communication at this stage prevents delays later in the process.

## Phase 5: Finishing, Quality Assurance, and Handover

The final phase brings the design to life and ensures every detail meets the Rea Co Homes standard of excellence.

### A. Installation of Finishes

Drywall is hung, taped, and painted. Then, the detailed work begins: installing custom cabinetry, laying luxurious flooring (hardwood, stone, or carpet), tiling bathrooms and kitchens, and installing trim and doors. This is where the personalized luxury of your custom home truly shines.

### B. Final Landscaping and Exterior Details

Exterior work, including final grading, driveway paving, and professional landscaping, is completed. Whether it’s high-desert xeriscaping or manicured lawns appropriate for a community like **Broken Top**, the exterior presentation is crucial.

### C. Quality Assurance and Homeowner Orientation

Kevin Rea’s commitment to quality is evident in our rigorous final inspection process. We conduct a comprehensive walk-through, addressing any minor imperfections. Finally, we provide a detailed homeowner orientation, explaining the operation of all systems, smart home technology, and maintenance requirements. We don't just hand over keys; we hand over a fully understood, functional, and flawless home.
