## Demystifying the Costs: How Much Does It Cost to Build a Custom Home?

The cost of building a custom home in Central Oregon is not a one-size-fits-all answer. It's influenced by a myriad of factors, making it essential to understand the variables involved. Generally, custom home costs are calculated per square foot, but this can vary widely based on:

*   **Location:** Building in highly sought-after communities like Tetherow, Brasada Ranch, or Pronghorn will typically incur higher land costs and potentially stricter architectural guidelines, which can influence overall project expenses.
*   **Size and Complexity:** Larger homes naturally cost more, but intricate architectural designs, unique features, and complex engineering (e.g., building on challenging terrain) also add significantly to the price.
*   **Materials and Finishes:** This is one of the biggest cost drivers. High-end finishes, custom cabinetry, smart home technology, energy-efficient systems, and premium materials (e.g., imported stone, bespoke millwork) can substantially increase the budget. A basic custom home might start at $350-$450 per square foot, while a luxury custom build with premium finishes could easily exceed $700-$1000+ per square foot in Central Oregon.
*   **Site Preparation:** Depending on the lot, costs for excavation, grading, utility hookups, and landscaping can vary. Difficult terrain or extensive tree removal will add to these expenses.
*   **Permits and Fees:** Local building permits, impact fees, and architectural review board fees (common in planned communities) are necessary expenses that must be factored in.

When you work with an experienced builder like Kevin Rea, you'll receive a detailed breakdown and realistic budget projections, ensuring there are no surprises along the way.

## The Timeline: How Long Does It Take to Build a Custom Home?

Building a custom home is a journey, not a sprint. While every project is unique, here's a general breakdown of the timeline you can expect:

*   **Design Phase (3-6 months):** This initial stage involves working with architects and designers to finalize floor plans, elevations, and material selections. This is a crucial period for decision-making and can be extended if changes are frequent.
*   **Permitting Phase (2-4 months):** Once designs are complete, plans must be submitted to local authorities (e.g., Deschutes County, City of Bend) for approval. This phase can vary significantly depending on the municipality's workload and the complexity of the project.
*   **Construction Phase (12-24 months):** This is where your home takes shape. The actual construction duration depends heavily on the home's size, complexity, weather conditions (especially in Central Oregon's winters), and the availability of skilled tradespeople. A typical custom home might take 12-18 months, but larger or more intricate projects can extend beyond 20 months.

From groundbreaking to handing over the keys, the entire process can range from 18 months to over 30 months. An experienced builder like Rea Co Homes will provide a realistic schedule and keep you informed at every stage.
