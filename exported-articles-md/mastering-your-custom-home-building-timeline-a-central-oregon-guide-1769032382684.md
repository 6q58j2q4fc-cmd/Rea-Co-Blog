# Mastering Your Custom Home Building Timeline: A Central Oregon Guide

Building a custom luxury home in Central Oregon—whether nestled in the high desert of Bend, overlooking the mountains at Tetherow, or enjoying the exclusivity of Brasada Ranch—is a dream realized through meticulous planning. One of the most common questions we receive at Rea Co Homes is: "What is the typical **custom home building timeline**?"

While every bespoke project is unique, especially when dealing with the specific regulatory and environmental considerations of Oregon, understanding the general phases is essential for setting realistic expectations. Led by Kevin Rea, who brings over 45 years of award-winning experience to every foundation poured, we believe transparency in the **construction schedule Oregon** is key to a stress-free process.

## Phase 1: Planning and Design (3–6 Months)

Before any dirt is moved, the most critical phase is planning. This stage determines the scope, budget, and ultimate success of your luxury home project. This is also the answer to **when to start building custom home**—the planning starts now, even if construction is months away.

### A. Discovery and Budgeting

We begin by understanding your vision, lifestyle, and financial parameters. For luxury builds in locations like Pronghorn or Sunriver, initial budgeting is complex and requires expertise to account for high-end materials and site-specific challenges (like challenging terrain or HOA requirements).

### B. Architectural Design and Engineering

Working with your chosen architect (or one recommended by Rea Co Homes), we move from conceptual sketches to detailed blueprints. This includes structural engineering, mechanical plans, and interior design specifications. This stage often involves several revisions to ensure every detail meets your exacting standards.

### C. Permitting and Approvals

This is often the most variable part of the timeline. Securing necessary permits from Deschutes County and specific approvals from HOAs (common in communities like Caldera Springs or Broken Top) can take significant time. Our team manages this complex process, leveraging our deep local relationships to expedite approvals wherever possible.

## Phase 2: Pre-Construction and Site Preparation (1–2 Months)

Once permits are in hand, we transition to preparing the site for construction.

### A. Site Work and Utilities

This involves clearing the land, grading the site, establishing temporary utilities, and ensuring proper erosion control measures are in place, which is particularly important given Central Oregon’s climate and environmental regulations.

### B. Final Selections and Procurement

While construction is about to begin, we finalize all major selections (windows, doors, roofing, HVAC systems) and initiate the procurement process. For a luxury build, materials often have long lead times, making early ordering essential to prevent delays later in the **custom home building timeline**.

## Phase 3: Construction – The Build Phase (10–18 Months)

This is the core construction period. The duration of **how long to build luxury home** depends heavily on complexity, size, and material availability, but 10 to 18 months is a realistic range for a high-end custom residence in Central Oregon.

### A. Foundation and Framing (2–3 Months)

Pouring the foundation, waterproofing, and erecting the structural frame. This phase progresses quickly and dramatically changes the landscape of your property.

### B. Rough-Ins (2–3 Months)

Installation of all internal systems: electrical wiring, plumbing pipes, and HVAC ductwork. This must be done precisely before insulation and drywall.

### C. Exterior Finishes (2–4 Months)

Installation of roofing, exterior siding (often incorporating natural materials like stone and cedar common in Central Oregon architecture), windows, and exterior doors. Achieving a weather-tight structure is critical for protecting the interior work.

### D. Interior Finishes and Detailing (4–6 Months)

This is where the luxury truly takes shape. Installation of drywall, interior trim, cabinetry, flooring, tiling, painting, and fixtures. High-end custom work requires meticulous attention to detail and craftsmanship, which Kevin Rea’s award-winning team is known for.

## Phase 4: Finalization and Move-In (1–2 Months)

### A. Punch List and Inspections

We conduct thorough final inspections with the county and create a detailed punch list to address any minor imperfections. Quality control is paramount to Rea Co Homes.

### B. Landscaping and Hardscaping

Installation of driveways, patios, decks, and landscaping, often focusing on drought-tolerant and native Central Oregon plants.

### C. Orientation and Handover

We walk you through your new home, explaining all systems and features. The keys are handed over, marking the successful completion of your **construction schedule Oregon**.

## Summary of the Custom Home Building Timeline

| Phase | Estimated Duration |
| :--- | :--- |
| Planning & Design | 3–6 Months |
| Pre-Construction & Site Prep | 1–2 Months |
| Construction (The Build) | 10–18 Months |
| Finalization & Handover | 1–2 Months |
| **Total Estimated Timeline** | **14–28 Months** |
