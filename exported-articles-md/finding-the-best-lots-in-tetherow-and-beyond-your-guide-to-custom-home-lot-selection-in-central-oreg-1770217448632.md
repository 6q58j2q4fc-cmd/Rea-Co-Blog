### 2. Topography and Orientation

Understanding the land's natural contours is vital. A sloped lot might offer incredible views but could increase construction costs. The orientation of the lot relative to the sun is also crucial for natural light, energy efficiency, and maximizing outdoor living spaces. Kevin Rea's 45+ years of experience allows him to quickly assess a lot's potential and challenges, guiding clients toward the most advantageous choices.

### 3. Views and Privacy

What kind of outlook are you hoping for? Mountain peaks, golf course greens, riverfront, or dense forest? Consider not just the current views, but also potential future developments that could impact them. Privacy is another key element; evaluate neighboring properties and potential sightlines.

### 4. Utilities and Infrastructure

Before committing to **buying land Central Oregon**, verify the availability and cost of connecting to essential utilities: water, sewer/septic, electricity, gas, and internet. Remote lots might require significant investment in infrastructure, which can impact your overall budget.

### 5. Zoning, Covenants, and Restrictions (CC&Rs)

Every lot comes with specific zoning regulations and, in planned communities, Covenants, Conditions, and Restrictions (CC&Rs). These rules dictate everything from building setbacks and height limits to architectural styles and landscaping requirements. Rea Co Homes is adept at navigating these complexities, ensuring your custom home design complies with all regulations while still achieving your vision.

### 6. Geotechnical Considerations

Understanding the soil composition and geological stability of the land is paramount. A geotechnical report can identify potential issues like expansive soils, rock formations, or drainage concerns that could impact foundation design and construction costs. Rea Co Homes always recommends thorough due diligence in this area.
