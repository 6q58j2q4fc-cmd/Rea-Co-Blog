# The Definitive Custom Home Building Timeline: Planning Your Luxury Home in Central Oregon

Building a custom luxury home in Central Oregon is an exciting journey, but it requires meticulous planning and a clear understanding of the **custom home building timeline**. Whether you are dreaming of a modern retreat in Bend, a sprawling estate in Brasada Ranch, or a golf-front masterpiece in Tetherow or Pronghorn, knowing **when to start building custom home** is the first step toward success.

At Rea Co Homes, we specialize in translating complex visions into reality. Kevin Rea brings over 45 years of award-winning experience to every project, ensuring a streamlined and predictable process, even amidst the unique challenges of the Oregon high desert climate. 

## Phase 1: Pre-Construction and Design (3–6 Months)

This initial phase is arguably the most critical for setting the foundation for your **construction schedule Oregon**. Rushing the design process often leads to costly changes later on.

### Site Evaluation and Selection
If you haven't already secured land, this is the time. We work with clients in locations like Bend, Redmond, Sisters, and exclusive communities to assess topography, utility access, zoning restrictions, and environmental considerations. This evaluation heavily influences the final design and budget.

### Architectural Design and Engineering
This is where your vision takes shape. Working with your architect (or one recommended by Rea Co Homes), we move from conceptual sketches to detailed blueprints. This phase includes:

*   **Schematic Design:** Initial layouts and elevations.
*   **Design Development:** Selecting major materials, systems, and finishes.
*   **Construction Documents (CDs):** Detailed drawings and specifications required for permitting.

### Permitting and Approvals
Obtaining necessary permits from local jurisdictions (Deschutes County, City of Bend, or specific homeowner associations like those at Brasada Ranch) can be variable. This process can take anywhere from 6 weeks to 3 months, depending on the complexity and current workload of the governing bodies. A well-prepared set of CDs, managed by an experienced builder like Rea Co Homes, minimizes delays.

## Phase 2: Procurement and Site Preparation (1–2 Months)

Once permits are secured, the physical work begins. However, before the first shovel hits the dirt, we focus on procurement—a vital step in determining **how long to build luxury home**.

### Material Ordering and Scheduling
Supply chain issues remain a factor, especially for high-end, custom materials (windows, specialized HVAC systems, custom cabinetry). We proactively order long lead-time items during the permitting phase to ensure they arrive on site exactly **when to start building custom home** components.

### Site Work and Foundation
This involves clearing the lot, establishing temporary utilities, and digging the foundation. Given Central Oregon's volcanic soil and varying terrain, precision in site preparation is paramount. The foundation pour marks the official start of the main construction timeline.

## Phase 3: Construction and Framing (4–6 Months)

This phase sees rapid vertical progress and is often the most exciting for homeowners.

### Framing and Roofing
The structure of your home is erected. Our award-winning team ensures structural integrity and adherence to the precise specifications outlined in the plans. Once the home is dried-in (roof and exterior sheathing complete), the interior work can begin, mitigating weather risks common in the high desert.

### Mechanical, Electrical, and Plumbing (MEP)
Rough-ins for all systems are installed within the walls, floors, and ceilings. This includes complex wiring for smart home technology, specialized plumbing for luxury fixtures, and efficient HVAC systems tailored for Oregon’s climate.

### Exterior Finishes
Siding, stone veneer, exterior doors, and windows are installed, further weatherproofing the structure.

## Phase 4: Interior Finishes and Detailing (3–5 Months)

This is the detailing stage, where the true luxury and custom nature of your home shine. This phase requires careful coordination to maintain the **custom home building timeline**.

*   **Insulation and Drywall:** Walls are sealed and prepared for paint.
*   **Cabinetry and Trim:** Custom millwork, built-ins, and trim are installed.
*   **Flooring and Tile:** Installation of hardwood, tile, and specialized flooring materials.
*   **Painting and Wall Coverings:** Interior painting and finishes.
*   **Fixture Installation:** Plumbing and lighting fixtures, appliances, and hardware are installed.

## Phase 5: Final Inspection and Move-In (1 Month)

The final stretch involves ensuring every detail meets our rigorous standards and passing all necessary final inspections.

### Punch List and Quality Assurance
Kevin Rea personally oversees the final quality assurance walk-through. A detailed “punch list” addresses any minor adjustments needed. This commitment to perfection is why Rea Co Homes has maintained its reputation for excellence in Central Oregon for decades.

### Landscaping and Hardscaping
Final grading, driveway paving, and landscaping are completed, integrating the home seamlessly into the natural beauty of the Central Oregon environment.

### Closing and Handover
Once all inspections are complete and the final payment is processed, we hand over the keys and provide a comprehensive orientation to your new custom home and its sophisticated systems.

## Summary of the Custom Home Building Timeline

For a complex, high-end luxury home in Central Oregon, the total **custom home building timeline** typically ranges from **12 to 20 months** from the start of design to move-in. The construction phase itself usually runs between 10 to 14 months, depending heavily on the complexity of the design and the current permitting climate.
