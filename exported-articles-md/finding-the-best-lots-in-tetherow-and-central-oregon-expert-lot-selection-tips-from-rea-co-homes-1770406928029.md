### Key Considerations for Lot Selection

Once your vision is clear, it's time to delve into the practical aspects of lot selection. This is where Kevin Rea's deep understanding of Central Oregon's unique geography and regulations truly shines.

#### 1. Topography and Grade

The slope and contour of a lot significantly impact construction costs and design possibilities. A very steep lot might offer incredible views but could require extensive excavation, retaining walls, and specialized foundation work, driving up costs. A relatively flat or gently sloping lot can be more cost-effective to build on. Consider how the natural grade will influence drainage and landscaping.

#### 2. Soil Conditions

Beneath the surface, soil conditions are paramount. Expansive clay soils, rocky terrain, or areas with high water tables can all pose challenges. Rea Co Homes always recommends a thorough geotechnical report to understand the soil composition. This prevents costly surprises during excavation and ensures a stable foundation for your home.

#### 3. Utilities and Infrastructure

Access to essential utilities—water, sewer (or septic), electricity, natural gas, and high-speed internet—is non-negotiable. In established communities like Tetherow or Brasada Ranch, these are typically readily available. However, when **buying land Central Oregon** in more rural areas, you might need to factor in the cost of extending utility lines, drilling a well, or installing a septic system. These can be significant expenses.

#### 4. Zoning and Building Codes

Every parcel of land is subject to specific zoning ordinances and building codes that dictate what can be built, where it can be placed, and even the architectural style. These regulations can affect setbacks, maximum height, lot coverage, and even the type of materials you can use. Kevin Rea and his team are experts in navigating these local regulations, ensuring your custom home design complies with all requirements in Bend, Sisters, Redmond, and beyond.

#### 5. Sun Exposure and Orientation

Central Oregon is known for its abundant sunshine. Understanding the sun's path across your lot throughout the day and year is vital for optimizing natural light, energy efficiency, and outdoor living spaces. Proper home orientation can maximize passive solar heating in winter and minimize heat gain in summer, while also ensuring your patios and decks get the ideal amount of sun or shade.

#### 6. Access and Easements

Consider how you will access your property. Is there a paved road, or will you need to build a driveway? Are there any easements (e.g., for utilities, shared driveways, or public access) that might affect your buildable area or privacy? It's crucial to review the property's title report and survey to understand all encumbrances.
