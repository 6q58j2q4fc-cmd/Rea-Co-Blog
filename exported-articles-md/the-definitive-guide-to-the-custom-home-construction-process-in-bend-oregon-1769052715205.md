# The Definitive Guide to the Custom Home Construction Process in Bend, Oregon

Central Oregon, with its stunning landscapes in Bend, Redmond, Sisters, and exclusive communities like Brasada Ranch, Tetherow, and Pronghorn, is the perfect backdrop for a custom-built home. However, for many prospective homeowners, the idea of embarking on a **home construction process Oregon** can feel daunting. 

At Rea Co Homes, we believe in demystifying the journey. As a premier **custom home builder Bend**, we guide our clients through every phase, ensuring a smooth, predictable, and exciting experience. Led by Kevin Rea, who brings over 45 years of award-winning experience to every project, we’ve perfected a streamlined process designed for excellence. 

If you are asking, “**How long to build custom home**?” the answer depends on complexity, but understanding the steps is key to setting realistic expectations. Here is our comprehensive guide to the **building timeline custom home** construction typically follows.

## Phase 1: Discovery, Design, and Pre-Construction (The Foundation of the Dream)

This initial phase is arguably the most critical for success and typically takes 3 to 6 months, depending on the complexity of the design and permitting requirements in Central Oregon.

### 1. Initial Consultation and Site Evaluation

We begin by meeting with you to discuss your vision, lifestyle needs, budget, and desired location (whether it’s a lot in Bend’s Westside or acreage near Sisters). We also conduct a thorough site evaluation. Understanding the unique topography, solar orientation, and utility access of your specific lot is vital, especially in areas like Tetherow or Pronghorn, which have specific design guidelines.

### 2. Design and Architectural Drawings

Working alongside our trusted architects and designers, we translate your vision into detailed blueprints. This stage involves numerous revisions to finalize floor plans, elevations, and material selections. We focus on integrating sustainable and energy-efficient elements appropriate for the high desert climate of Central Oregon.

### 3. Permitting and Engineering

Once the design is finalized, we submit the plans to the relevant local authorities (Deschutes County or the City of Bend). Permitting times can vary significantly, but our deep local knowledge helps us navigate the requirements efficiently. Simultaneously, we finalize structural engineering, geotechnical reports, and secure financing.

## Phase 2: Site Preparation and Foundation (Breaking Ground)

With permits secured, the physical **home construction process Oregon** officially begins. This phase usually takes 4 to 8 weeks.

### 1. Site Clearing and Grading

We clear the site, establish the construction entrance, and grade the land to ensure proper drainage. This is a critical step, especially on sloped lots common in the high desert.

### 2. Utilities and Excavation

Trenches are dug for the foundation and utilities (water, sewer, electric). The foundation area is excavated according to the engineering plans. 

### 3. Footings and Foundation

Forms are built, rebar is placed, and concrete is poured for the footings and slab or basement walls. Once the concrete cures, waterproofing is applied, and the foundation is inspected.

## Phase 3: Framing and Rough-Ins (The Structure Takes Shape)

This is where the structure rapidly becomes recognizable, typically taking 8 to 12 weeks.

### 1. Framing

The skeleton of your home—the walls, floors, and roof trusses—is erected. This is a moment of high excitement as you walk through the spaces for the first time. Our focus on precision framing ensures structural integrity for decades to come.

### 2. Roofing and Siding

Once the structure is “dried in,” the roof is installed, followed by exterior sheathing, house wrap, windows, and doors. This protects the interior from Central Oregon’s weather.

### 3. Rough-Ins (Mechanical, Electrical, Plumbing)

All the hidden systems are installed: plumbing pipes, HVAC ductwork, electrical wiring, and low-voltage systems. This stage requires meticulous coordination among trades, something Kevin Rea’s decades of experience ensures is managed flawlessly.

## Phase 4: Interior Finishes and Details (Bringing the Vision to Life)

This phase focuses on aesthetics and functionality, typically spanning 10 to 16 weeks.

### 1. Insulation and Drywall

Insulation is installed (often exceeding standard requirements for energy efficiency), followed by drywall. Once the drywall is taped, sanded, and textured, the interior begins to feel like a home.

### 2. Interior Finishes

This stage includes painting, installing trim, doors, cabinetry, and built-ins. We work with local artisans to ensure the highest quality finishes, whether it’s custom millwork for a home in Brasada Ranch or bespoke cabinetry for a modern Bend residence.

### 3. Flooring, Tile, and Countertops

Hard surfaces are installed, including flooring, custom tile work in bathrooms and kitchens, and stone countertops. Fixtures (plumbing and lighting) are also set.

## Phase 5: Final Touches and Completion (Handing Over the Keys)

The final stretch typically takes 4 to 6 weeks.

### 1. Exterior Finishes and Landscaping

Final grading, driveway paving, decks, patios, and landscaping are completed, enhancing the curb appeal and integrating the home with the natural Central Oregon environment.

### 2. Final Inspections and Walkthrough

We conduct rigorous internal quality control checks before scheduling final municipal inspections. Once approved, we walk through the home with you, creating a punch list of any minor adjustments. This is your opportunity to learn about the operation of all systems.

### 3. Closing and Move-In

Once the punch list is complete and final payment is made, we hand over the keys and warranty information. Your dream home, built by the finest **custom home builder Bend**, is ready for you.

## Understanding the Building Timeline Custom Home

So, **how long to build custom home** with Rea Co Homes? While every project is unique, a typical, moderately complex custom home in Central Oregon takes approximately **10 to 16 months** from the start of design to move-in. The construction phase itself (Phase 2 through 5) usually lasts 6 to 10 months, depending on weather, complexity, and permitting speed.
