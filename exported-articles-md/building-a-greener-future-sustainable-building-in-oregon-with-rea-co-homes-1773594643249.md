## What Makes a Home Sustainable?

When we talk about **eco-friendly custom homes**, we're referring to a suite of practices and features designed to minimize environmental impact and maximize efficiency. Here are some key elements we integrate into our projects:

### Energy Efficiency: The Core of Green Living

Perhaps the most impactful aspect of sustainable building is energy efficiency. Our **energy efficient homes Bend** are designed to significantly reduce heating, cooling, and electricity consumption. This includes:

*   **Superior Insulation:** Utilizing advanced insulation techniques and materials to create a tightly sealed building envelope, minimizing thermal loss.
*   **High-Performance Windows and Doors:** Installing energy-star rated windows and doors that prevent heat transfer and improve indoor comfort.
*   **Efficient HVAC Systems:** Incorporating high-efficiency heating, ventilation, and air conditioning systems, often paired with smart thermostats for optimal control.
*   **Renewable Energy Integration:** Exploring options like solar panels to generate clean energy, further reducing reliance on traditional power grids.
*   **Passive Solar Design:** Strategically orienting homes to maximize natural light and solar heat gain in winter, while minimizing unwanted heat in summer.

### Thoughtful Material Selection

The materials used in construction play a crucial role in a home's sustainability profile. At Rea Co Homes, we prioritize:

*   **Locally Sourced Materials:** Reducing transportation emissions and supporting local economies by using materials from nearby suppliers whenever possible.
*   **Recycled and Reclaimed Content:** Incorporating materials with recycled content, such as recycled steel, composite decking, or reclaimed wood for unique architectural features.
*   **Sustainable Wood Products:** Utilizing sustainably harvested timber and engineered wood products that are efficient in their use of resources.
*   **Low-VOC Products:** Selecting paints, sealants, and finishes with low or no volatile organic compounds (VOCs) to ensure superior indoor air quality and reduce harmful off-gassing.

### Water Conservation Strategies

Water is a precious resource, especially in the high desert environment of Central Oregon. Our sustainable homes incorporate features to conserve water both inside and out:

*   **Low-Flow Fixtures:** Installing toilets, showerheads, and faucets designed to use less water without sacrificing performance.
*   **Efficient Irrigation Systems:** Implementing smart irrigation systems for landscaping that respond to weather conditions and plant needs, or utilizing drought-tolerant native landscaping (xeriscaping).
*   **Rainwater Harvesting:** Where feasible, integrating systems to collect and store rainwater for irrigation or other non-potable uses.

### Waste Reduction and Healthy Indoor Environments

Minimizing construction waste and ensuring a healthy living space are also key components of our approach:

*   **Construction Waste Management:** Implementing rigorous recycling programs on job sites to divert waste from landfills.
*   **Enhanced Indoor Air Quality:** Beyond low-VOC materials, we focus on proper ventilation strategies to introduce fresh air and exhaust pollutants, creating a healthier environment for occupants.
