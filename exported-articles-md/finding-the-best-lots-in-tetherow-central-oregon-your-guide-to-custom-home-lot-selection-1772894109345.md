## Key Considerations for Custom Home Lot Selection Tips

Navigating the Central Oregon real estate market for land can be complex. Here are critical factors to consider when **buying land Central Oregon**:

### 1. Location, Location, Location

Central Oregon offers a variety of distinct communities, each with its own charm and amenities. Are you drawn to the vibrant energy of Bend, the exclusive golf communities of Tetherow and Pronghorn, or the serene ranch lifestyle of Brasada Ranch? Your desired lifestyle will heavily influence your ideal location. Consider proximity to schools, work, recreation (skiing, hiking, fishing), and essential services.

### 2. Topography and Terrain

Examine the slope, elevation, and natural features of the lot. A flat lot might seem easier to build on, but a gently sloping lot can offer opportunities for walk-out basements, enhanced views, and creative landscaping. Be mindful of significant slopes, rock outcroppings, or wetlands, as these can impact construction costs and timelines. Kevin Rea's 45+ years of experience in Central Oregon means he understands how the unique geology of the region affects building.

### 3. Views and Orientation

Central Oregon is renowned for its panoramic vistas, from the Cascade Mountains to the high desert. If **view lots Bend Oregon** are a priority, assess what views are available from different points on the lot and how they might be impacted by future development or existing trees. Consider the sun's path throughout the day and year. Proper home orientation can maximize natural light, passive solar heating, and shade, significantly impacting comfort and energy efficiency.

### 4. Utilities and Infrastructure

Verify the availability and proximity of essential utilities: water, sewer/septic, electricity, natural gas, and high-speed internet. Undeveloped lots may require significant investment in bringing these services to the property line, which can add substantial costs to your project. Understanding these requirements upfront is crucial for accurate budgeting.

### 5. Zoning and Covenants, Conditions, & Restrictions (CC&Rs)

Before falling in love with a lot, thoroughly investigate its zoning regulations. These dictate what can be built on the property, including setbacks, height restrictions, and allowable uses. In planned communities like Tetherow, Pronghorn, or Brasada Ranch, CC&Rs are equally important. These private rules govern everything from architectural style and exterior materials to landscaping and even fence heights, ensuring community aesthetic consistency. Rea Co Homes is intimately familiar with the specific CC&Rs of Central Oregon's premier communities.

### 6. Soil Conditions and Geotechnical Reports

Soil stability is paramount for a solid foundation. In some areas, poor soil conditions may require extensive and costly remediation, such as specialized foundations or retaining walls. A geotechnical report provides crucial information about the soil composition and recommendations for foundation design. This is an investment that can save you significant headaches and expenses down the line.
