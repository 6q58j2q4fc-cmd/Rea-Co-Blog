## Custom Home Lot Selection Tips: Key Considerations

Choosing the right lot goes far beyond location and price. A successful build depends on how well the land integrates with the home design. Here are essential **custom home lot selection tips** we share with our clients:

### 1. Understanding Topography and Site Prep

The slope of the land significantly impacts construction costs. A flat lot is generally easier and cheaper to build on, but a sloped lot might offer superior views. When evaluating a lot:

*   **Drainage:** Where does water naturally flow? Poor drainage can lead to costly foundation issues down the road.
*   **Soil Conditions:** Central Oregon soil can range from volcanic rock to sand. Geotechnical reports are crucial to determine the required foundation type. Excavating solid rock, for instance, can significantly increase site prep costs.
*   **Tree Coverage:** While beautiful, excessive tree removal can be expensive and may be restricted by local ordinances or HOA rules, especially in areas like Tetherow.

### 2. Maximizing Views and Orientation

If you are seeking **view lots Bend Oregon**, the orientation of the lot is everything. The sun’s path dictates natural light, energy efficiency, and view corridors.

*   **Southern Exposure:** Ideal for maximizing natural light and passive solar gain, which is excellent for energy efficiency during Central Oregon's cold winters.
*   **Western Views:** Often provide spectacular sunset views, but require careful window placement and shading to mitigate intense afternoon heat.
*   **Cascade Views:** Most desirable lots face west or southwest to capture the stunning mountain range. Kevin Rea's team specializes in designing homes that frame these iconic views perfectly.

### 3. Analyzing Covenants, Conditions, and Restrictions (CC&Rs)

Many of the prime communities in Central Oregon—including Tetherow, Brasada, and Pronghorn—have strict CC&Rs. These rules dictate everything from home size and exterior materials to landscaping and construction timelines. Before committing to **buying land Central Oregon**, ensure the CC&Rs align with your vision for the home. A lot might seem perfect until you realize the covenants prohibit the modern design you dreamed of.

### 4. Infrastructure and Utilities

Verify the availability and proximity of essential utilities:

*   Water and Sewer/Septic
*   Electricity and Gas
*   Internet/Cable (Crucial in more remote areas)

If the lot is undeveloped, the cost of bringing utilities to the site must be factored into your budget. This is particularly important when looking at acreage outside city limits.
