## Essential Criteria: What to Look for in a Luxury Builder

Choosing a builder is not just about comparing bids; it's about evaluating competence, communication, and compatibility. Focus on these key areas:

### 1. Portfolio and Specialization

Review their portfolio. Do their past projects reflect the quality and style you envision? A builder specializing in luxury custom homes (like those found in Tetherow or Brasada Ranch) will have established relationships with high-end subcontractors and suppliers, ensuring access to premium materials and superior execution. Look for builders who demonstrate versatility while maintaining consistent quality.

### 2. Financial Transparency and Process

Luxury building requires meticulous financial management. The builder must be transparent about costs, change orders, and timelines from the outset. A reputable custom builder will utilize detailed contracts and provide clear communication regarding budgets. Avoid builders who offer vague estimates or pressure you into quick decisions.

### 3. Communication and Team Chemistry

Your relationship with your builder will last 12 to 18 months, sometimes longer. Open, honest, and frequent communication is vital. Assess how quickly they respond to inquiries and their willingness to collaborate with your architect and design team. The best builders act as partners, guiding you through the complex process with patience and expertise.

## Questions to Ask Custom Builder Candidates

Preparation is key. Before signing a contract, ensure you have clear answers to these critical **questions to ask custom builder** candidates. These inquiries help distinguish a competent builder from a truly exceptional one:

*   **Experience and Licensing:** “How many custom homes have you built in Central Oregon, specifically in areas like Bend or Sisters?” (Verify their licensing and insurance.)
*   **Project Management:** “How do you manage the construction schedule, and what tools do you use to keep clients updated on progress and budget?”
*   **Subcontractor Relationships:** “Can you provide references from your key subcontractors? How long have you worked with them?” (Long-term relationships indicate reliability and quality control.)
*   **Warranty and Service:** “What does your warranty cover, and what is your process for addressing issues after the home is completed?”
*   **Client References:** “Can I speak with your three most recent clients, and perhaps one client from five years ago?” (Older references speak volumes about long-term satisfaction and build quality.)
*   **Design-Build Capabilities:** “Do you offer design-build services, or how do you prefer to collaborate with my chosen architect?”
