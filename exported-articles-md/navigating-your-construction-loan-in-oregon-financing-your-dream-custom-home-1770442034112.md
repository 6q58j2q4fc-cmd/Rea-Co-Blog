### Understanding Construction Loans: The Foundation of Your Build

A construction loan is fundamentally different from a traditional mortgage. Instead of providing a lump sum upfront, these loans are disbursed in stages, known as 'draws,' as construction milestones are met. This protects both the lender and the borrower, ensuring funds are used appropriately and work progresses as planned. For your custom home in Central Oregon, a construction loan is typically a short-term loan that covers the costs of land acquisition (if not already owned), materials, and labor during the building phase.

**Key features of construction loans:**

*   **Interest-Only Payments:** During construction, you typically pay interest only on the funds drawn, not the entire loan amount.
*   **Draw Schedule:** Funds are released based on a pre-approved schedule tied to construction progress, verified by inspections.
*   **Conversion Options:** Many construction loans can convert into a permanent mortgage once the home is complete, often called a 'construction-to-permanent' loan.

### Custom Home Mortgage Options: Beyond the Build

Once your magnificent custom home is complete, the construction loan needs to be paid off. This is where **custom home mortgage options** come into play. For many homeowners, a construction-to-permanent loan is the most seamless route. This single loan closes once, saving you time and closing costs by avoiding a separate application and closing for the permanent mortgage.

Alternatively, you can opt for a two-loan approach: a standalone construction loan followed by a separate traditional mortgage once construction is finished. The best option for you will depend on your financial situation, credit score, and market interest rates at the time of conversion. Our team at Rea Co Homes can connect you with trusted local lenders who specialize in these unique financing structures for custom builds in areas like Bend and Sisters.

### Luxury Home Financing: Tailored Solutions for Premium Builds

Building a luxury custom home in Central Oregon's most prestigious communities – think Brasada Ranch, Tetherow, or Pronghorn – often requires specialized **luxury home financing**. These properties typically involve higher price points, unique architectural designs, and premium finishes, which can sometimes fall outside the parameters of conventional loan limits.

**Considerations for luxury home financing:**

*   **Jumbo Loans:** For loan amounts exceeding conventional limits (which vary by county), jumbo loans are necessary. These often have stricter credit requirements and may require larger down payments.
*   **Portfolio Lenders:** Some banks and credit unions offer in-house portfolio loans, which can be more flexible for unique, high-value properties that don't fit standard underwriting guidelines.
*   **Asset-Based Lending:** For high-net-worth individuals, lenders might consider a broader range of assets beyond traditional income for qualification.
