Tetherow is one of Bend's most sought-after destinations, known for its dramatic golf course, stunning Cascade Mountain vistas, and proximity to Phil's Trail network. Securing the **best lots in Tetherow** requires speed, local knowledge, and an understanding of the community's Design Review Board (DRB) requirements.

**Key Considerations for Tetherow Lots:**

1.  **View Corridors:** Not all lots offer unobstructed views of the Three Sisters or Broken Top. Work with a builder like Rea Co Homes who understands how to position a home to maximize these precious views while adhering to height restrictions.
2.  **Topography:** Steep lots might offer better views but increase foundation and construction costs. A flat lot might be easier to build on but limit architectural options.
3.  **Setbacks and Build Envelope:** Tetherow has specific rules regarding where you can place a structure. Knowing the buildable area before purchasing is non-negotiable.

## Essential Custom Home Lot Selection Tips

Choosing the land is often more complex than choosing finishes. Here are the top **custom home lot selection tips** Kevin Rea shares with his clients:

### 1. Understand the Site’s Utilities and Infrastructure

Do not assume all services are readily available, especially when **buying land Central Oregon** outside of established city limits. 

*   **Water and Sewer:** Is the lot on city services, or will you need a well and septic system? Drilling a well can be costly and unpredictable in certain areas.
*   **Power and Gas:** How far away are the nearest hookups? Running power lines a long distance can significantly impact your budget.
*   **Internet:** For those working remotely, confirming access to high-speed internet is essential, particularly in rural areas like Brasada Ranch or remote parts of Redmond.

### 2. Analyze Topography and Soil Conditions

The slope of the land dictates the style of home you can build (e.g., walk-out basement vs. single-level ranch) and affects drainage. More importantly, the soil type is critical.

Central Oregon often features volcanic rock, which requires extensive blasting or specialized excavation—a major unexpected cost. Before closing on the land, Rea Co Homes always recommends a geotechnical survey to assess soil stability and composition. This proactive step prevents costly surprises down the road.

### 3. Maximize Sun Exposure and Orientation

Central Oregon is famous for its sunshine. Strategic lot orientation is key to energy efficiency and comfort. 

*   **Passive Solar Gain:** Orienting the main living areas and large windows toward the south can maximize natural light and warmth during the winter.
*   **Shade and Overheating:** Placing bedrooms and utility rooms on the north side can keep them cooler. A skilled builder will know how to position the home on the lot to mitigate the intense summer sun while still capturing those desirable **view lots Bend Oregon** offers.

### 4. Review Covenants, Conditions, and Restrictions (CC&Rs)

In planned communities like Tetherow, Pronghorn, or Brasada Ranch, CC&Rs dictate everything from architectural style and exterior materials to landscaping and fence height. These rules ensure community cohesion but can restrict your design freedom. Review them thoroughly to ensure they align with your vision. Kevin Rea’s team works closely with DRBs to streamline the approval process, ensuring your custom design meets all requirements efficiently.
